ABOUT

Some choices we made as we depicted Copeland’s path to radicalization — spoilers ahead!

Generally, there are difficulties that come with “gamifying” historical events. We cannot say with certainty what would have happened so we simply take creative liberties. The pathway to radicalization is also very complex in the first place, there are an immeasurable amount of factors in real life.

But for the purpose of this game, our outcomes are generally determined by an invisible “regret” index—representing doubt, empathy, or positive influence determined by the number of sympathetic choices (such as not indulging homophobic thoughts early on, reacting empathetically to the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, reaching out to a teacher, and so on) the player chooses. There are a total of five outcomes, as follows:


1. "The adults in your life are able to provide you with the foundations for a good support system. It gives you the courage to become more confident and outgoing. You don’t let the bullies bother you. You are able to build lasting connections, follow good role models, and find support when you need it. As an adult, you become well integrated into society."

This is the (best) outcome achieved when there is early intervention—you reach out to a teacher and are able to receive support down the line when arriving at conflicting feelings. (This second option is only visible to you if you sought help in the first place, demonstrating a willingness to establish connection.) In reality however, during this period it was noted that the staff at school had no recollection of Copeland, as if he were invisible (Ryan 2004). This, among other indicators of social isolation, we consider to be one of the more important push factors in Copeland’s radicalization, so later choices do not have as significant of an effect on the hypothetical path we’ve drawn out for Copeland.


2. "You continue to be active in the BNP and eventually run for office. You leave after the 2004 election year after the party wins zero seats in Parliament again. You feel disillusioned by far-right politics."

The outcome explores if Copeland had stuck with legitimate means of addressing his grievances. However, disillusionment is still a risk factor and does not disqualify him from eventual violence.


3. "You continue to work at the London Underground for the rest of your life. You feel unhappy with the people around you, but do little to change it. Just a cog in the machine."

This outcome explores if Copeland had never continued to pursue means of addressing his grievances after leaving the BNP. The option to leave the NSM, which directly leads to this outcome, is only available if the player has achieved a certain threshold of regret. His participation in the NSM was crucial and laid the foundations to turn his frustrations towards minority communities and embrace white supremacist and violent ideals. Also around this time, he engaged with resources such as the Anarchist Cookbook, which gave him the knowledge to assemble his bombs. He may have acted alone, but he would not have been able to carry out these attacks without the influence of this network. If this outcome (and all previous outcomes) are not achieved, we consider this (for the purpose of this game) to be a point of no return where Copeland carries out the bombings regardless. After sticking with the NSM he is entrenched in ingroup vs outgroup ideals, and the outgroup must be destroyed for the survival of the ingroup.


4. “Despite your history of mental illness, the court rules that you are still fully liable for your crimes. You receive six life sentences. While in prison, you attack another inmate and receive an additional three years added to your sentence. You also threaten to assassinate a Spice Girl because she is friends with gay people. After several years, you convert to Islam to atone for what you have done and demand that other inmates refer to you as Saddam, in reference to the dictator Saddam Hussein."

This is the original outcome for Copeland.


5. "In prison, you learn about a deradicalization program that involves education about racism, extremism, and terrorism, and making official apologies to the communities hurt. Through this program, you learn about the organization Life After Hate and become a member. You become involved in P/CVE through advocacy and teaching young British people about your story. You hope that learning that there is a path out will help others in similar situations and prevent further tragedies."

This outcome explores if Copeland had grapped with regret or doubt throughout his radicalization, despite following through with his terrorist actions. This outcome is only achieved by acknowledging his regrets at the end, which is only possible if the player has made a number of sympathetic choices so far. While it is too late for Copeland, we imagine that offenders with regret and genuine willingness to atone could join deradicalization efforts and advocate for P/CVE.
